AEI · Internal Brief · for Capgemini leadership

The category Capgemini is positioned to claim — but only if we move now.

The strategic-decision quadrant of decision intelligence is empty and just got validated by Gartner. The competitor in the operational quadrant is venture-scale. Capgemini already has the relationships, the credibility and the buyer access AEI needs to land — the channel exists; the product now exists too. This brief sets out why AEI is the move, the four stages to hunt the xbn-EUR market, the three structural paths, and what it takes to get going.

01Why · What · How

Why Capgemini, in one page.

AEI productizes the highest-margin work Capgemini already delivers — strategic advisory at the board level — into a recurring intelligence subscription with bound artifacts. The work is the same; the economics are not.

Why
The channel already exists.
CFO, Chief Strategy Officer and board access is the bottleneck for every player chasing this category. Capgemini sits in those rooms today, quarter after quarter. That access cannot be bought by an independent venture; it can be activated by a CG-backed product. The window is the eighteen months before someone else names the category.
Channel · credibility · relationships
What
A persistent intelligence subscription.
Not a consulting engagement, not a dashboard. A live workbench plus a monthly or quarterly bound briefing pack, anchored to a six-dimension score that is updated weekly against the customer's own systems and a constructed peer cohort. Recurring revenue, 80%+ gross margin, sticky by the artifact ritual itself.
Recurring · bound · scored
How
Wedge to annuity.
Capgemini account teams open the door; the consulting diagnosis is the wedge; the subscription is the annuity. The same buyer relationship that produced one project becomes a multi-year intelligence contract priced between €300K and €800K per year — with platform expansion paths into PE portfolios and the broader Innovation Edition.
Wedge · subscribe · expand

04Strategic options · three structural paths

There are three plausible ways Capgemini takes AEI to market. Each is honestly drawn on the same axes — how fast we hunt the xbn market against the durability of the strategic position we build. The third path is the one that reaches both.

Path A
AEI as a CG service offering
AEI delivered project-by-project as a high-end advisory engagement. Fast revenue, familiar shape — but no platform moat, no recurring revenue, no category claim.

07Marketing plan

No slides. Working software, bound artifacts, named conversations.

The category will be named in the next eighteen months. The firm that ships the first bound artifact into a real boardroom — not the firm with the loudest content marketing — gets to define it. AEI's marketing motion mirrors the product itself: artifact-first, sober, hard to fake.

Layer 1 · the wedge
Artifact-led demand
Every prospect interaction ends with an artifact they can hold or click. The live workbench in the room; the bound Onboarding Folio after signing; the quarterly Board Briefing Pack on the renewal cycle. Demand generated by the artifact, not the deck.
· Live demos on the customer's own data
· Bound Onboarding Folio at signing
· Quarterly Briefing Pack shipped physically
Layer 2 · the category
Define the language first
The category does not yet have a name. We claim "strategic decision intelligence" — the natural adjacency to the operational quadrant Gartner just validated — in long-form essays, briefings to two named analysts per quarter, and a single annual "State of Strategic Decisions" report.
· Two analyst briefings per quarter
· One annual state-of-category report
· Editorial essays in two outlets (HBR, Sifted)
Layer 3 · the room
Closed events · not stages
Three intimate annual events — CFO dinners, board chair roundtables — co-hosted with Capgemini, where the workbench is run live against an anonymized cohort. The right twelve people in a room outperforms a thousand at a stage.
· 3 closed events per year · <20 seats each
· Board-chair roundtables in DACH, UK, US
· PE GP dinners in London & New York

No paid advertising. No outbound SDR motion. The artifact is the brand — and the channel is the relationship.
